school Motto

'Shramayeva Jayate' We at Little Angels believe that hard work is the key to success. Hard work always triumphs - this mantra for success is instilled in the young minds. They are taught that they need the right attitude and self discipline to achieve success. We constantly endeavor to make every child a complete child by making him /her believe that there is no substitute for hard work, and more importantly the happiness a child derives from working hard, on reaching his/her goal is priceless.

Mission

Little Angels is committed to believe that every child in the school is important for the family, the state and the nation and has an inborn intelligence to grow to become a productive human resource for the development of the country. The school is an institution which should take care of growth of all the faculties of children through basic education till 10th class and endeavor to make every child a complete child by making him believe that he has to grow to become an autonomous, self-disciplined and responsible.

What's new

  • Endurance Run-2020

    To encourage physical fitness and team spirit the School has a well defined games programme. Seasonal games include cricket, hockey, football, athletics and swimming. Minor games include basketball, tennis, volleyball, skating and which are played around the year.Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays are reserved for games. All games played culminate into house matches with points being allotted to the winning house. A Cock House Cup is awarded, at the end of the term to the House which has secured the maximum points. Regular gymnastics, boxing and endurance building training programme are held throughout the year.

  • about meals

    The School lays a lot of emphasis on hygienic and nutritious food necessary for a growing child. For this the school has taken the services of a dietician who not only supervises the food but also lays the menu. The school provides vegetarian and non – vegetarian food. Mutton / chicken / egg curry is provided four times a week. For vegetarians it is paneer / mushrooms which is served four times a week. Every meal is accompanied by a fruit or a dessert/ ice – cream. Dal, rice, chapattis, vegetables, salads and curd are a regular feature.
